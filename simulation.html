Time Evolution

Time evolution in quantum mechanics describes how a particle’s wavefunction changes with time according to the Schrödinger equation. For a particle in a box, each energy eigenstate evolves with a specific phase factor, but a superposition of states leads to a time-dependent probability distribution. This results in observable dynamics such as oscillations and shifting probability densities within the box. The study of time evolution highlights the role of interference between states and provides insight into quantum dynamics beyond stationary solutions. It is essential for understanding real quantum systems and forms a bridge toward concepts used in quantum computing and qubit manipulation.
