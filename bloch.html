Bloch Sphere

The Bloch sphere provides a geometric representation of a qubit, allowing its quantum state to be visualized as a point on the surface of a sphere. The north and south poles correspond to the basis states |0⟩ and |1⟩, while all other points represent superposition states defined by two angles.
This representation makes it easier to understand how phase and amplitude determine the state of a qubit. Quantum operations can be interpreted as rotations of the state vector on the sphere, offering an intuitive way to study quantum gates and transformations. The Bloch sphere thus serves as a powerful tool for connecting abstract quantum concepts with visual and physical intuition.
