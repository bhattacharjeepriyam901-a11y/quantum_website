Particle in a Box

The particle in a box model is one of the fundamental problems in quantum mechanics that illustrates how microscopic particles behave under confinement. Unlike classical particles, a quantum particle confined within rigid boundaries can only occupy discrete energy levels, leading to energy quantization. The wavefunction describing the particle must satisfy boundary conditions, resulting in standing wave patterns inside the box. This model helps in understanding important quantum concepts such as probability density, nodes, and normalization. It also forms the basis for more advanced systems, including quantum wells, nanostructures, and ultimately qubits in quantum computing.
